ABOUT THE TEAM:

Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) is a franchise cricket team that represents the city of Bangalore (now called Bengaluru) in the Indian Premier League (IPL). The team was founded in 2008 and is owned by United Spirits, a subsidiary of the British alcoholic beverages company Diageo. It was named after the company's liquor brand Royal Challenge. The Bangalore franchise was purchased by Vijay Mallya, who paid US$111.6 million for it. The team's home ground is the M. Chinnaswamy Stadium in Bengaluru, which has a seating capacity of around 35,000. The team's logo is a red lion with a golden mane, and its jersey is predominantly red with black and gold accents. RCB has had some big-name players in its squad over the years, including Indian cricketing legend Rahul Dravid, as well as current Indian captain Virat Kohli, and South African batting sensation AB de Villiers. RCB has set records for both the highest and lowest totals in the IPL, scoring 263/5 as their highest score, and 49 as their lowest score. The team has also had some famous coaches, including Gary Kirsten and Daniel Vettori. Despite being one of the most popular teams in the IPL, RCB has had a mixed record in the tournament, having finished runners-up on three occasions (2009, 2011, and 2016) but failing to win the title so far. However, the team has a reputation for playing an exciting brand of cricket and has some of the most loyal fans in the tournament.

Rahul Dravid was the captain of RCB in the first three seasons of the IPL, from 2008 to 2010. RCB finished runners-up in the 2009 IPL, losing to Deccan Chargers in the final. It was their best performance in the tournament under Dravid's captaincy. Dravid was known for his calm and composed captaincy style, and he was respected by his teammates and opponents alike.Although Dravid did not have a lot of success as RCB's captain in terms of trophies, his leadership helped build the foundation of the team, and his contribution to RCB's journey in the IPL has been significant.


VIRAT KOHLI'S ERA (2013-2021)

Virat Kohli has been one of the most successful cricketers in the Indian Premier League (IPL). He has played for the Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) since the inception of the tournament in 2008. Kohli is known for his aggressive batting style and his ability to consistently score runs in the IPL. Virat Kohli has been the captain of RCB since 2013, and under his leadership, the team has had brilliant results in the IPL. Virat remains one of the most popular and successful captains in the IPL, and his leadership has played a crucial role in RCB's performances over the years. Kohli has also been a consistent performer for RCB over the years, scoring over 500 runs in six different IPL seasons. He is the only batsman to have scored more than 500 runs in three consecutive seasons (2015-2017) and is among the top five run-scorers in the tournament's history. Kohli's success in the IPL has earned him the reputation of being one of the greatest T20 batsmen of all time. His ability to perform under pressure and lead his team from the front has made him a fan favorite and an inspiration for aspiring cricketers all over the world.

RCB has qualified for the playoffs four times under Virat's captaincy (in 2015, 2016, 2019, and 2020). RCB finished runners-up in 2016 under Virat's captaincy, losing to Sunrisers Hyderabad in the final. Virat Kohli has been one of the most successful batsmen in the IPL, and he has scored the most runs in a single season (973 runs in 2016) and has won the Orange Cap (awarded to the leading run-scorer of the tournament) twice under his captaincy. RCB under Virat's captaincy holds the record for hitting the most number of sixes in an innings (21 sixes against the Gujarat Lions in 2016), and the highest team total in IPL history (263/5 against Pune Warriors India in 2013). RCB has been one of the most consistent teams in the IPL under Virat's captaincy, finishing in the top four in the league stage in five out of the eight seasons he has led the team.
